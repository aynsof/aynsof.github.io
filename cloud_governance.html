Radiating Information

Teams that are autonomous must not be black boxes. They must radiate information to all stakeholders. This should include information about delivery, security, cost, system health, product usage metrics.

When we talk about ‘radiating information’, we’re not talking about writing reports. That kind of manual reporting can be a huge drain on delivery teams. We’re talking about things like kanban boards, which show work in progress and highlight blockers. Or something like Google Analytics, which can show user activity. Or the security tools that are built into public cloud providers for owners to get insights in real time into their security posture.

Executive Involvement

Teams need to radiate information. The other essential part of the equation is that leaders must pay attention to this information. They must look at it, understand it (at some level of granularity) and be able to interrogate it. This needs to be the case at all levels of seniority. Red lights mean nothing if they don’t raise questions.

What Good Looks Like

As an example, during the deployment of the Australian Bureau of Statistics’ Australian Marriage Law Postal Survey system, the ABS’s Chief Operating Officer was at daily standups to see the work being performed. When blockers were identified, he was able to make calls and immediately remove these obstacles. The system was eventually deployed in less than a hundred days, which would be a great achievement in any organisation - in a public service agency, it is quite incredible.
